The Last of Us part I

The drama chronicles a pandemic future that has been devastating for humanity, leaving humans on the verge of extinction. The fungus turns people into c4nn1b4ls and spreads quickly with a single bite. A few decades later, the few survivors that remained were living traveling or in quarantines protected by government officials. This is the scenario in which we follow Joel (Pedro Pascal), a tough survivor who is hired to take young Ellie (Bella Ramsey) out of the oppressive quarantine zone where she lives. The two must find a group of paramilitary rebels who have rebelled against the authorities. However, they discover on the way that Ellie is possibly infected with the virus, despite the young woman not showing the recurrent symptoms of the disease. Because of this, they believe that Ellie's immunity could be the key to finding a cure for the fungus and potentially saving humanity.

The Last of Us Part II

The Last of Us Part II takes place five years after the final events of the first game. Ellie is 19 years old and lives in a community on the outskirts of Jackson, Wyoming, in the northwestern United States. The settlement is integrated by several survivors and life there is prosperous, despite the circumstances. Infected are still a problem in the world and patrols are recurring to ensure the safety of residents against them and against people with bad intentions.
Ellie is in her prime, maturing her personality and wanting to be more independent. The relationship with Joel became delicate due to the events that led the former smuggler to do what he did five years ago, at the Fireflies base in St. Mary's Hospital in Salt Lake City, which made the fact become an extremely sensitive subject within their relationship.
